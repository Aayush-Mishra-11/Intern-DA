================================================================================
                        5. HYPOTHESIS TESTING
================================================================================

TEST 1: Monthly Charges by Churn Status
---------------------------------------
Type: Independent Two-Sample T-Test
H0: No difference in monthly charges between churned and non-churned
H1: Significant difference exists

Results:
  - T-statistic: 2.4102
  - P-value: 0.016303
  - Significant: YES (α = 0.05)

Conclusion: Customers who churn have significantly higher monthly charges
than those who don't churn.


TEST 2: Tenure by Churn Status
------------------------------
Type: Independent Two-Sample T-Test
H0: No difference in tenure between churned and non-churned
H1: Significant difference exists

Results:
  - T-statistic: -13.2034
  - P-value: < 0.000001
  - Significant: YES (α = 0.05)

Conclusion: Customers who churn have significantly shorter tenure than
those who don't churn. This is a critical finding for retention strategies.


TEST 3: Sales by Product Category
---------------------------------
Type: One-Way ANOVA
H0: All product categories have equal mean sales
H1: At least one category differs

Results:
  - F-statistic: 2.0508
  - P-value: 0.093428
  - Significant: NO (α = 0.05)

Conclusion: No significant difference in sales across product categories.
This suggests balanced revenue distribution across products.


TEST 4: Monthly Charges vs Hypothesized Mean
--------------------------------------------
Type: One-Sample T-Test
H0: Population mean = $100
H1: Population mean ≠ $100

Results:
  - T-statistic: 5.8863
  - P-value: < 0.000001
  - Significant: YES (α = 0.05)

Conclusion: Average monthly charges ($113.64) is significantly higher than
the hypothesized $100.


TEST 5: East vs West Regional Sales
-----------------------------------
Type: Independent Two-Sample T-Test
H0: No difference between East and West regions
H1: Significant difference exists

Results:
  - T-statistic: 2.0202
  - P-value: 0.049619
  - Significant: YES (α = 0.05)

Conclusion: There is a significant difference in sales between East and
West regions, with East showing higher sales.
